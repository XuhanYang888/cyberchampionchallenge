Risks of AI

Cybersecurity Risks of Artificial Intelligence


Concerns


Data Security and Privacy

AI systems often gather and analyze massive amounts of general and personal data from the user. From collected data and activity logs, AI can infer sensitive information, such as income, address, preferences, etc. This data can be used by businesses for marketing insights or sold to other companies, potentially leading to privacy breaches and unauthorized data use.


Identity theft

AI technologies pose significant cybersecurity risks through identity theft through video and voice generation. These synthetic media technologies can be used to create fake scenes indistinguishable to real ones. With such tools, hackers are able to bypass authentications, ultimately leading to financial exploitation and more.


Bias and Misinformation

The dependence on AI tools for decision-making could potentially lead to a lack of critical thinking and creativity. The reliance on AI technologies also means the possibility for AI systems to perpetuate existing biases and misleading information, or provide the user with completely detrimental suggestions. For example, if an commonly used AI system had been manipulated to deliberately reduce awareness around certain cyberattack techniques, many people may become vulnerable to cybersecurity breaches.

AI-based algorithms can influence what information people are provided with. If its training datasets have been maliciously manipulated, AI technologies could be used to spread misinformation and impact our everyday lifes. This includes societal perceptions, economical trends, political standpoints, etc.
